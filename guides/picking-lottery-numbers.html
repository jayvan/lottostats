Picking Lottery Numbers

There are lots of resources that claim they have strategies for picking numbers that are better than others. The truth is, the best thing you can do is not pick! I’ll explain why having the lottery terminal pick your numbers is the best strategy, and debunk some common myths.

Human Bias

We’re all human, and when we pick our own numbers we are susceptible to human bias. If you pick your birth month, day, favourite player’s jersey, or lucky numbers like 7, 11, and 21 you’re being biased. Even if you draw a certain shape or pattern on the selection card, you’re being biased!

The truth is, each and every combination of numbers is equally likely to be drawn in the lottery, so no combination is better than any other combination. If your numbers are chosen though, remember that you have to share the prize with everybody else that picked those same numbers. Take the number combination: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 for example. That combination is chosen by 767 people for Lotto MAX every single week. If the Jackpot was $60,000,000 and the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 were chosen, each player would walk away with a measly $78,226! The lottery terminals use an unbiased random number generator, therefore, never pick your own numbers. If you are buying combination tickets you have to fill out a selection slip. In this case use a random number generator to select your numbers, don’t fill it out yourself, or you’ll just increase your odds of sharing. I recommend random.org for generating random numbers.

Hot & Cold Numbers

Most lottery results come from a ball drop machine, though more and more are using secure random number generators because they are less expensive to purchase and operate. The theory of hot and cold numbers is that certain numbers come up more or less often than others. A number is considered HOT if it has come up often in recent draws. People think that if a number has come up recently, it’s going to keep coming up because of the physical properties of the ball in the drop machine, or because of flaws in the random number generator. On the other end we have COLD numbers, which have not come up lately. Like hot numbers, people think that cold numbers are less likely to come up so they shouldn’t be chosen. Alternatively, cold numbers may be “due for a win”, so they should be chosen.

The truth is, lottery balls are regularly audited to make sure that they are exactly the same size and weight. Similarly, the random number generators are audited extensively to make sure that they generate winning numbers fairly. By selecting or avoiding hot and cold numbers you are just falling prey to another kind of human bias, making yourself more likely to share a prize if the numbers are chosen. Remember that every number has an equal chance of being selected.
